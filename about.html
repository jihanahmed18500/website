Journey.

Hi, I’m Jihan Ahmed. My path hasn’t followed the typical trajectory, and that’s exactly what prepared me to lead.

01. The Foundation

Before I ever stepped into a traditional classroom full-time, I spent years focused on memorizing the Qur’an and completing my Hifz. That experience was deeply meaningful to me and taught me discipline, patience, and commitment at a young age.

But it also meant that when I eventually entered the regular school system, I started later than most of my peers and had years of academic content to catch up on. Walking into school already behind was intimidating.

02. The Acceleration

Instead of letting the gap discourage me, I decided to treat it as motivation. I worked hard to close the gaps, often learning things my peers had studied years earlier while trying to keep up with current coursework at the same time.

I completed Grades 9 and 10 in the same year, and am now finishing four years of high school in three.

It hasn’t been easy. Catching up on years of missed content required long hours and a willingness to constantly push myself. But that experience taught me resilience and showed me that the path to success doesn’t have to look the same for everyone.

03. Advocacy & Action

I’ve always believed that education should go beyond just grades. At SATEC @ W.A. Porter, I’ve focused on initiatives that support student wellbeing—most notably helping organize a school-wide Mental Health Day for over 1,200 students.

Leadership has also been a major part of my journey through the Equity Council, the Muslim Students’ Association (MSA), and Model United Nations (MUN). Whether promoting inclusion or negotiating global policy, these roles have sharpened my ability to speak about complex challenges facing the world.

Setbacks are just setups.

Today, I carry the lessons from every stage: the discipline from Hifz, the perseverance from academics, and the leadership from community work. Together, they shape the way I approach challenges and the impact I hope to make.

My story is still unfolding—and I’m just getting started.
